# Professional Communication Skills

## Topic
Effective Communication Strategies

## Key Points
- "Know, Relate, Understand" Approach
- How to ask customers questions
- Active listening
- Keeping the Customer Call Focused

## Explanation

## "Know, Relate, Understand" Approach
A simple communication technique that helps helpdesks' to communicate with customers by:
1. Learn how the customer wants to be addressed.
2. Make them feel comfortable and reassured. Tell them that you will do your best to resolve the issue.
3. Match explanation to their level of understanding.

## How to ask customers questions
Use both open-ended and close-ended questions:
1. Ask relevant questions – Use open-ended questions first to understand the problem clearly.
2. Check the scope – Use closed-ended questions to find out who or what is affected.
3. Check when it started – Use closed-ended questions to confirm when the issue began or occurs.
4. Check if constant or random – Use closed-ended questions to know if it always happens or only sometimes.
5. Check for recent changes – Use open-ended questions to ask if anything changed before the issue started.
6. Narrow down causes – Use both open and closed-ended questions to compare what works and what does not.

## Active Listening
Let the customer explain the situation while listening, Occassionally reply with "I understand" and "Yes", After they're done explaining, 
summarize the customer's problem and verify if your understaning is correct then clarifying questions.

## Keeping the Customer Call Focused

- Purpose
Control the conversation and use time efficiently while solving the issue.

- Best Practices
1. Use proper language – Speak clearly and avoid technical terms the customer may not understand.
2. Listen and question – Let the customer explain, then use open-ended and closed-ended questions to gather details.
3. Give feedback – Show that you understand the issue and maintain a friendly, positive tone.
4. Adjust to customer behavior – Customers may be confused, frustrated, angry, or inexperienced. Use active listening to identify this.
5. Stay professional – Remain calm, do not take comments personally, and never argue or criticize.
6. Keep focus on resolution – Guide the conversation back to solving the problem.

- Difficult Customers
Some customers may show multiple difficult traits. Handle them professionally by staying calm, listening well, and keeping control of the call.

## Summary
In summary, this topic is about how to effectively apply appropriate communication strategies to ensure that:
1. The customer feels comfortable and reassured.
2. We are able to gather relevant information regarding the customer's issue. (Using proper open and close ended questions)
3. We are able to properly handle different types of customers.
4. We are able to control the call and focus on solving the issue.
